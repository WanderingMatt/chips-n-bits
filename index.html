Halo Peer Pressure

by Matthew Anderson

I consider myself pretty fortunate. I was 21 years old before anyone tried to get me to do it for the first time. “Everyone is doing it”, they said. I remember how nervous I felt, walking up their driveway for the very first time. Once Inside, the true nature of the location was revealed. 4-10 guys. One television upstairs, one downstairs. The entire lot, hunched around their designated light source screaming, groaning and sweating. Random outbursts of anger. Pizza and Mountain Dew everywhere. How had I gotten myself in to this situation?

Peer pressure. It’s not just for candy anymore, children. It’s an epidemic in the world of video games and, arguably the most successful marketing campaign for several popular franchises. My story is just one example of how it can ruin, then subsequently enrich your life.

Halo: Combat Evolved

I didn’t purchase the original. I didn’t even own an Xbox at the time. I convinced myself that I could get by with just a taste now and then. I can’t explain why I played so much of the game. The story had potential, but I didn’t end up caring about a single character. The game took place on these fabulous planets, yet the level design felt claustrophobic. Multiplayer was undoubtedly the most enjoyable part of the game and it was still handcuffed by my biggest gripe - your character moved so slow. All I could ever think about while playing was, “I wish I had a mouse”. The only thing that really impressed me were the innovative (at the time) vehicle controls. And yet, I kept coming back to it.
